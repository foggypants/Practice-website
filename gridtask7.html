Why are template areas easy to understand?

They use descriptive names like "header" and "footer," making the layout visual and readable directly in the CSS code.

Which section spans full width?

The sections where the name is repeated across all columns, such as "header header" or "footer footer," span the full width.

Can we rearrange layout by changing area names?

Yes, you can swap the positions of the names in the grid-template-areas property to move sections without touching the HTML.

Is this better than column numbers?

It is often better for complex layouts because you don't have to keep track of line numbers or math; you just draw the map with text.

Where are template areas mostly used?

They are mostly used for high-level website skeletons involving a header, main content, sidebar, and footer.
